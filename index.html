Learn the Fundamentals

This site introduces basic basketball skills for beginners: ball-handling, shooting form, and simple defensive principles. Use these tips and drills to build confidence and improve your game one practice at a time.

Start Training

Dribbling

Use your fingertips, keep the ball low, and keep your eyes up. Strong dribbling lets you control the game instead of the ball controlling you.

Shooting

Focus on balance, elbow under the ball, and a smooth follow-through. Good form matters more than distance when you are starting out.

Defense

Stay low in a stance, slide your feet, and stay between your player and the basket. Great defense creates easy offense.

Why Fundamentals Matter

Every great player started with the basics. Practicing simple, repeatable skills helps you build muscle memory so that dribbling, shooting, and defense feel natural during games.
