Dribbling Basics

Start with a low athletic stance, knees bent and back straight. Use your fingertips instead of your palm and keep the ball between your knee and waist. Practice with both hands so you can dribble confidently on either side of the court.

  • Stationary pound dribbles (right and left hand)
  • Low, medium, and high dribbles
  • Simple crossovers from hand to hand

Shooting Form

Square your feet to the basket and keep them shoulder-width apart. Balance the ball on your shooting hand and use your guide hand on the side. Bend your knees, lift the ball in one smooth motion, and finish with a relaxed wrist and high follow-through.

  • Form shooting close to the basket
  • One-hand shooting (guide hand off the ball)
  • Spot-up shots from different areas

Defensive Fundamentals

A good defender stays low and balanced, with active hands. Focus on the offensive player’s chest or hips instead of the ball. Slide your feet instead of crossing them so you can react quickly.

  • Defensive stance holds
  • Slide drills from sideline to sideline
  • Mirror drills with a partner

Sample Practice Plan

Here is an example 60-minute workout based on these fundamentals.

Time Focus Drill
0–10 minutes Warm-up Light jog, dynamic stretching
10–25 minutes Dribbling Stationary dribbles, crossovers, zig-zag dribbles
25–40 minutes Shooting Form shooting, spot-up shots, free throws
40–55 minutes Defense Stance holds, slide drills, close-outs
55–60 minutes Cool Down Light stretching and recap
